excerpt: 'Urban structures create heat zones that impact the immunity of city-dwelling wildlife. We studied how habitat temperature and feather color polymorphism in Santiago's pigeons (Columba livia) affect physiological traits like immunoglobulin Y and leukocyte profiles, while accounting for sex and seasonality.

The results show that pigeons in warmer urban areas have higher H/L ratios (a stress indicator), an effect influenced by body mass. Additionally, humoral immunity was shaped by the interaction between environmental temperature, plumage melanism, and sex. These findings highlight how thermal heterogeneity and physical traits drive the immune responses of birds living in modified landscapes.'
